# Employee Attrition Analysis
> A Business Analytics project analyzing IBM HR data to identify key drivers of employee attrition.

## Problem Statement
The organization faces a **16.12% attrition rate** — 1 in 6 employees are leaving. This project identifies the root causes and provides data-driven HR recommendations.

## Key Findings
| Factor | Insight |
|--------|---------|
| Overall Attrition | 16.12% (237 out of 1,470 employees) |
| Highest Risk Dept | Sales — 20.63% attrition rate |
| Overtime Impact | Overtime employees are 3x more likely to leave (30.53% vs 10.44%) |
| Salary Gap | Departed employees earned ₹2,045 less per month on average |
| Age Group | Employees aged 25–35 show highest attrition |

## Recommendations
1. Salary review for Sales and HR departments
2. Overtime cap policy (max 10 hrs/week)
3. Sales retention program with career tracks
4. Mentorship program for employees aged 25-30
